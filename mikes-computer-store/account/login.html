Mikes Computers PIXEL POLICY

Due to the nature of the manufacturing process, occasional defects can occur from the manufacturer. Pixel defects or failures cannot be fixed or repaired and can happen at any stage of a product’s life. Based on the acceptable visible defective pixels in your Mikes Computers notebook or All-in-One, the part will be determined as defective and replaceable upon request, if within the warranty period.

What is a pixel?

Short for picture element, a pixel is a single point in a graphic image. Mikes Computers laptops and All-in-One PCs display pictures by dividing the display screen into millions of pixels, arranged in rows and columns. The pixels are placed close together so that they appear connected. A full pixel is a combination of one red, one green, and one blue sub-pixel.

What are pixel defects?

A pixel defect is a bright white dot or very noticeable black dot on the display. Bright dot sub-pixel defects are permanently "on", displaying a colored red, green, or blue spot on the display at all times. Dark dot sub-pixel defects are permanently "off", displaying a black dot on the display at all times.

How long does this policy apply?

The Mikes Computers Pixel Policy is in effect during the system’s one (1) year, two (2) year or three (3) year Parts and Free Shipping warranty. Please review your invoice to determine which warranty you purchased.

What is acceptable in this policy?

Bright dot quantity: Maximum of 5 (two-adjoining Bright dots count as one)

Dark dot quantity: Maximum of 6 (three-adjoining Dark dots count as one)

Total Bright and Dark dots: Maximum of 8
